$100 annual DCA

Comparing scenarios: This page reports IRR because the $100 deposits have different holding periods (the first compounds for the full horizon, the last for just one year). The lump-sum page reports CAGR because all $1,000 is deployed at the window start. Both are annualized rates and fair to compare on a per-dollar-of-time-invested basis — but the two scenarios deploy different totals of capital over different effective durations (annual DCA contributes $100 × N years), so a higher DCA IRR can still leave you with less terminal money than a lower lump-sum CAGR. Compare the terminal-value chart and the summary table alongside the rate, not the rate alone.
