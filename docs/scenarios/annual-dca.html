$100 annual DCA

Comparing scenarios: This page reports IRR because the ten $100 deposits have different holding periods (the first compounds for 10 years, the last for 1). The lump-sum page reports CAGR because all $1,000 is deployed at once. Both are annualized rates and fair to compare on a per-dollar-of-time basis — but the lump sum keeps roughly 2× the dollar-years of capital invested across the window, so a higher DCA IRR can still leave you with less terminal money than a lower lump-sum CAGR. Compare the terminal-value chart and the summary table alongside the rate, not the rate alone.
